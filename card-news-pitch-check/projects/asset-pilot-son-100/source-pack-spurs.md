### Useful Text Snippets
- Cristian Stellini spoke about the role model qualities of Heung-Min Son after the South Korea star reached the 100-goal landmark in the Premier League in our 2-1 win against Brighton on Saturday.
- What a way to do it, as well. Sonny’s milestone goal was one of his best, shifting the ball away from Pascal Gross and Joel Veltman before bending a shot into the top corner from 25 yards.
- Sonny’s strike opened the scoring after nine minutes of a huge encounter at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium. Brighton arrived in N17 a place below us in sixth, four points adrift with games in hand, and levelled on 33 minutes when Lewis Dunk headed home Solly March’s corner. In form Seagulls impressed but we landed a vital victory when Harry Kane buried Pierre-Emile Hojbjerg’s cutback 12 minutes from time.
- Almost eight years after his first goal in the Premier League - the winner against Palace at the Lane on 20 September, 2015 - Sonny has become the first Asian player to reach three figures in the competition.
- “It was an amazing goal from Sonny,” Acting Head Coach Cristian told SPURSPLAY. “He scores a lot of these type of goals, and we are happy for him, 100 goals in the Premier League. We spoke many times about Sonny. We don’t have to forget that this is a long season full of things that have happened, the World Cup, but Sonny pushes himself every day 100 per cent. He is a player who you have to look at and learn the way to be professional and the way to be a great player.”
- Speaking in his press conference, Cristian added: “We celebrate into the dressing room. What I said was this week we celebrate 70 years of Gianni Vio, we celebrate a new birth in Ryan Mason's family, he has a new son and this type of human thing you have to celebrate every time. So, we also had 100 goals from Sonny. It's amazing to have this, it's normal that in football that when you win this type of match there are individual targets, but that target can create energy if our best players play for the team and for the win.”
